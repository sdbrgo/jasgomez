About Me

A Computer Science professional specializing in Computational Human Factors and Ergonomics research. My work centers on modeling and simulation of human–system interaction to support safer, more informed decision-making in complex environments. I am particularly interested in decision support systems, conceptual and simulation modeling, the computational representation of olfactory perception, and the human aspect in Industry 5.0. Through research-driven approaches, I aim to design and evaluate systems that optimize organizational processes while prioritizing human health, safety, and performance.
